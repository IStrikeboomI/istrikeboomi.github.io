Euler's Formula is:

$$e^{ix} = \cos x + i\sin x$$

Where \(e\) is Euler's number, \(i\) is the imaginary unit

And \(x\) is a real number representing an angle in radians.

Using Euler's formula, we can express complex numbers in polar form:

$$z = re^{i\theta}$$

Where \(r\) is the magnitude (or modulus) of the complex number

and \(\theta\) is the argument (or angle) in radians.

To convert from rectangular form to polar form, we use:

$$r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$

$$\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right)$$

To convert from polar form to rectangular form, we use:

$$x = r\cos\theta$$

$$y = r\sin\theta$$

Also $\text{cis}(x) = e^{ix} = \cos x + i\sin x$ is an alternate notation

Intuitive Meaning

$e^{i\theta}$ represents a rotation in the complex plane by an angle $\theta$ radians.

$\cos(\theta)$ represents the real part of the complex number $e^{i\theta}$.

$\sin(\theta)$ represents the imaginary part of the complex number $e^{i\theta}$.

This puts the real on the x axis and the imaginary on the y axis

Similar to the unit circle

Now in order to derive Euler's formula, you need calculus/series

But the consquences it leads to are important and calculus is not needed

Applications

We can rewrite $1,-1,i,-i$ in polar form:

$$1 = 1 \cdot e^{i \cdot 0}$$

$$-1 = 1 \cdot e^{i \cdot \pi}$$

$$i = 1 \cdot e^{i \cdot \frac{\pi}{2}}$$

$$-i = 1 \cdot e^{i \cdot \frac{3\pi}{2}}$$

And since there are exponentials, they have the same properties as exponentials

Like $e^{ia} \cdot e^{ib} = e^{i(a+b)}$ which means multiplying complex numbers adds their angles

And $\frac{e^{ia}}{e^{ib}} = e^{i(a-b)}$ which means dividing complex numbers subtracts their angles

Or with polar form:

$$r_1 e^{i\theta_1} \cdot r_2 e^{i\theta_2} = (r_1 r_2) e^{i(\theta_1 + \theta_2)}$$

$$\frac{r_1 e^{i\theta_1}}{r_2 e^{i\theta_2}} = \left(\frac{r_1}{r_2}\right) e^{i(\theta_1 - \theta_2)}$$

Also since $\cos(\theta)$ and $\sin(\theta)$ are periodic by $2\pi$, we have $e^{i(\theta + 2\pi)} = e^{i\theta}$

Conversion Between Rectangular and Polar Forms

To convert from rectangular form to polar form, we use:

$$r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$

$$\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right)$$

This is because $r$ is the distance from the origin to the point $(x, y)$

$\theta$ is the angle from the positive x-axis to that point.

To convert from polar form to rectangular form, we use:

$$x = r\cos\theta$$

$$y = r\sin\theta$$

This is because $x$ is the horizontal component

And $y$ is the vertical component.

De Moivre's Theorem

De Moivre's Theorem states that for any real number $n$:

$$\left(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta\right)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i \sin(n\theta)$$

This can be written more compactly using Euler's formula:

$$\left(e^{i\theta}\right)^n = e^{in\theta}$$

Or for a polar form complex number

$$\left(re^{i\theta}\right)^n = r^n e^{in\theta} = r^n\left(\cos(n\theta) + i \sin(n\theta)\right)$$

Practice Problems
  • Convert $5 + 2i$ to polar form

    $$\begin{align} r &= \sqrt{5^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{29} \\ \theta &= \arctan\left(\frac{2}{5}\right) \approx 0.3805 \text{ radians} \\ \text{Polar Form} &= \sqrt{29} e^{i \cdot 0.3805} \end{align}$$

  • Convert $e^{i\frac{\pi}{4}}$ to rectangular form

    $$\begin{align} x &= \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \\ y &= \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \\ \text{Rectangular Form} &= \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \end{align}$$

  • Convert $e^{i}$ to rectangular form

    $$\begin{align} x &= \cos(1) \approx 0.5403 \\ y &= \sin(1) \approx 0.8415 \\ \text{Rectangular Form} &= 0.5403 + i\cdot 0.8415 \end{align}$$

  • Use De Moivre's Theorem to compute $(\cos 30^\circ + i \sin 30^\circ)^3$

    $$\begin{align} (\cos 30^\circ + i \sin 30^\circ)^3 &= \cos(3 \cdot 30^\circ) + i \sin(3 \cdot 30^\circ) \\ &= \cos 90^\circ + i \sin 90^\circ \\ &= 0 + i \cdot 1 = i \end{align}$$

  • Solve $(2 + 9i)/(6 + 6i)$ by converting to polar form and then back to rectangular form

    $$\begin{align} \text{Rectangular Form:} & \quad \frac{2 + 9i}{6 + 6i} \\ \text{Polar Form of Numerator:} & \quad r_1 = \sqrt{2^2 + 9^2} = \sqrt{85}, \quad \theta_1 = \arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) \\ \text{Polar Form of Denominator:} & \quad r_2 = \sqrt{6^2 + 6^2} = 6\sqrt{2}, \quad \theta_2 = \arctan\left(1\right) = \frac{\pi}{4} \\ \text{Division in Polar Form:} & \quad \frac{r_1}{r_2} e^{i(\theta_1 - \theta_2)} = \frac{\sqrt{85}}{6\sqrt{2}} e^{i\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)} \end{align}$$

    Now converting back to rectangular form:

    $$\begin{align} x &= \frac{\sqrt{85}}{6\sqrt{2}} \cos\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) - \frac{\pi}{4}\right) \\ y &= \frac{\sqrt{85}}{6\sqrt{2}} \sin\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) - \frac{\pi}{4}\right) \end{align}$$

    This gives us

    $$\text{Rectangular Form} \approx 0.708 + 0.459i$$

  • Solve $(2 + 9i)\cdot(6 + 6i)$ by converting to polar form and then back to rectangular form

    $$\begin{align} \text{Rectangular Form:} & \quad (2 + 9i)(6 + 6i) \\ \text{Polar Form of First Factor:} & \quad r_1 = \sqrt{2^2 + 9^2} = \sqrt{85}, \quad \theta_1 = \arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) \\ \text{Polar Form of Second Factor:} & \quad r_2 = \sqrt{6^2 + 6^2} = 6\sqrt{2}, \quad \theta_2 = \arctan\left(1\right) = \frac{\pi}{4} \\ \text{Multiplication in Polar Form:} & \quad r_1 r_2 e^{i(\theta_1 + \theta_2)} = \sqrt{85} \cdot 6\sqrt{2} e^{i\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) + \frac{\pi}{4}\right)} \end{align}$$

    Now converting back to rectangular form:

    $$\begin{align} x &= \sqrt{85} \cdot 6\sqrt{2} \cos\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) + \frac{\pi}{4}\right) \\ y &= \sqrt{85} \cdot 6\sqrt{2} \sin\left(\arctan\left(\frac{9}{2}\right) + \frac{\pi}{4}\right) \end{align}$$

    This gives us

    $$\text{Rectangular Form} \approx -26 + 66i$$
