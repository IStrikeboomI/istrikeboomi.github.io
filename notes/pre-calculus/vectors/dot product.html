Vector dot product is defined with two vectors of the same length/dimension $n$

$$\begin{align} \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_1v_1 + u_2v_2 + u_3v_3 + ... + u_nv_n \end{align}$$

It can also be written in summation notation

$$\begin{align} \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \sum_{i = 1}^n{u_iv_i} \end{align}$$

There is also an alternate, trigonometric way of writing dot product ($\theta$ is the angle between the vectors, $ 0 \leq \theta \leq \pi$)

$$\begin{align} \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = ||\vec{u}||\cdot ||\vec{v}|| \cdot \cos(\theta) \end{align}$$

Using the above formula you can also find the angle between two vectors

$$\begin{align} \cos(\theta) = \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{||\vec{u}||\cdot ||\vec{v}||} \rightarrow \theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{||\vec{u}||\cdot ||\vec{v}||}\right) \end{align}$$

Properties

For the following $\vec{u}, \vec{v}, \vec{w}$ are vectors and $c$ is a constant

Commutative: $\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} = \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}$

Distributive: $(\vec{u} + \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{w} = \vec{u} \cdot \vec{w} + \vec{v}\vec{w}$

Constant Distributive: $c(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}) = c\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \vec{u} \cdot c\vec{v}$

Magnitude: $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u} = u_1^2 + u_2^2 + u_3^2 + ... u_n^2 = ||\vec{u}||^2$

It's important to note that dot product is NOT Associative, which means $(\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u}) \cdot \vec{w} \neq \vec{v} \cdot (\vec{u} \cdot \vec{w})$

What Does This Mean?

Dot product can be thought of as how aligned the two vectors are

If we were to graph the two vectors then see how length to which they stay aligned, it would be the dot product

Another intrepretation is what amount of one vector goes in the direction of the other vector, scaled by the length of the other vector

Dot product can also result in a negative number, this just means the vector are pointing on opposite sides

Why Cosine?

The cosine formula can also be derived from the graph below

We can use the law of cosines

The length $c$ (hypotenuse) is the magnitude of $\vec{u}$

The length $a$ (adjacent) is the magnitude $\vec{v}$

The length $b$ (opposite) is another vector $\vec{u} - \vec{v}$ that is the differnce of the other two

$$\begin{align} ||\vec{u} - \vec{v}||^2 = ||\vec{u}||^2 + ||\vec{v}||^2 - 2||\vec{u}||||\vec{v}||cos(\theta) \end{align}$$

Using the magnitude property we can simplyfy the right side

$$\begin{align} ||\vec{u} - \vec{v}||^2 &= (\vec{u} - \vec{v}) \cdot (\vec{u} - \vec{v}) \\ &= \vec{u}(\vec{u} - \vec{v}) - \vec{v}(\vec{u} - \vec{v}) \\ &= \vec{u}\cdot\vec{u} - \vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} + \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} - \vec{v}\cdot\vec{v} \\ &= ||\vec{u}||^2 - 2\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} + ||\vec{v}||^2\\ \end{align}$$

We can plug this back in

$$\begin{align} ||\vec{u}||^2 - 2\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} + ||\vec{v}||^2 &= ||\vec{u}||^2 + ||\vec{v}||^2 - 2||\vec{u}||||\vec{v}||cos(\theta) \\ - 2\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} &= -2||\vec{u}||||\vec{v}||cos(\theta) \\ cos(\theta) &= \frac{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}}{||\vec{u}||||\vec{v}||} \end{align}$$

We can use the $||\vec{u}||\cdot ||\vec{v}|| \cdot \cos(\theta)$ formula to understand dot product

Since $||\vec{u}||$ and $||\vec{v}||$ are always positive (because length is positive), the sign of $cos(\theta)$ affects the dot product

If dot product is positive, it means cosine is positive, which means the angle is in $[0,\pi/2)$, so they are pointing the same direction

If dot product is 0, it means cosine (or a length) is 0, which means the angle is in $\pi/2$, so they are perpendicular

If dot product is negative, it means cosine is negative, which means the angle is in $(\pi/2,\pi]$, so they are pointing the same opposite direction

If dot product is 0, the vectors are called orthogonal (perpendicular)

Practice Problems
  • $\left< 2,5,2 \right> \cdot \left< 1,5,3 \right> $

    $$\begin{align} \left< 2,5,2 \right> \cdot \left< 1,5,3 \right> = 2\cdot 1 + 5\cdot 5 + 2\cdot 3 = 33 \end{align}$$

  • $\left< 10,2,4 \right> \cdot \left< 2,2,2 \right> $

    $$\begin{align} \left< 10,2,4 \right> \cdot \left< 2,2,2 \right> = 10\cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 2 + 4 \cdot 2 = 32 \end{align}$$

  • The length of 2 vectors are $4$ and $12$, the angle between them is $\theta = 35^\circ$, find the dot product

    $$\begin{align} 4 \cdot 12 \cdot \cos(35^\circ) = 48 \cdot 0.81915 = 39.3192 \end{align}$$

  • Find the angle between $\vec{v} = \left< 2,4 \right>$ and $\vec{u} = \left< 4,6 \right> $

    $$\begin{align} \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} = 2\cdot 4 + 4\cdot 6 = 32 \\ ||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{2^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{20} \\ ||\vec{u}|| = \sqrt{4^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{52} \\ \end{align}$$

    $$\begin{align} \theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{||\vec{u}||\cdot ||\vec{v}||}\right) = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{32}{\sqrt{20} \cdot \sqrt{52}}\right) \approx \cos^{-1}\left(0.992277876714\right) \approx 7.125^\circ \end{align}$$
