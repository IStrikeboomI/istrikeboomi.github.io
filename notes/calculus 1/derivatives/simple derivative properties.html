When it comes to solving derivatives, using the limit definition becomes impractical for complicated functions

Instead we can use various properties, here are 3 simple yet fundamental properties

Sum/Difference Property (Assuming $f'(x)$ and $g'(x)$ exist):

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[f(x) \pm g(x)\right] = \frac{d}{dx}\left[f(x)\right] \pm \frac{d}{dx}\left[g(x)\right]= f'(x) \pm g'(x)$$

Constant property ($c$ is a constant):

$$\frac{d}{dx} c = 0$$

Constant Coefficient Property ($c$ is a constant and $f'(x)$ exists):

$$\frac{d}{dx} cf(x) = c\frac{d}{dx} f(x) = cf'(x)$$

Power rule ($n$ is a constant):

$$\frac{d}{dx}x^n = nx^{n-1}$$

One common mistake people make with the power rule is they use a variable in the exponent, which is not allowed

The power rule only works for polynomials like $x^3$ or $x^{\frac{1}{2}}$ NOT $2^x$

Example:

$$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^4 + .25x^2 + 5x - \sqrt{x} - \frac{1}{x} + 10\right] \\ \frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^4\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[.25x^2\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[5x\right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[\sqrt{x}\right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[\frac{1}{x}\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[10\right] &&\text{Using Sum/Difference Property}\\ \frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^4\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[.25x^2\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[5x^1 \right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[x^{\frac{1}{2}}\right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[x^{-1}\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[10\right] &&\text{Rewrite using exponents}\\ 2\frac{d}{dx} \left[x^4\right] + .25\frac{d}{dx} \left[x^2\right] + 5\frac{d}{dx} \left[x^1 \right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[x^{\frac{1}{2}}\right] - \frac{d}{dx} \left[x^{-1}\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[10\right] &&\text{Factor out constants} \\ 2\left[4x^3\right] + .25\left[2x\right] + 5\left[1\right] - \left[\frac{1}{2}x^{-\frac{1}{2}}\right] - \left[-x^{-2}\right] + \left[0\right] &&\text{Apply Rules} \\ 8x^3 + \frac{1}{2}x + 5- \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} + \frac{1}{x^{2}} &&\text{Simplify} \\ \end{align}$$

Using each property indivually is pretty slow and long, so most people just condense these into one step

Proofs
  • Proof of Sum/Differnce Property

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} \left[f(x) \pm g(x)\right] &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{(f(x + h) \pm g(x + h)) -(f(x) \pm g(x))}{h}\right] \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{f(x + h) - f(x) \pm g(x + h) - \pm g(x)}{h}\right] &&\text{Rearrange terms}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{(f(x + h) - f(x)) \pm (g(x + h) - g(x))}{h}\right] &&\text{Factor out $\pm$}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}\right] \pm \lim_{h \to 0} \left[\frac{g(x + h) - g(x)}{h}\right] &&\text{Limit Sum/Difference Property}\\ &= f'(x) \pm g'(x) &&\text{Definition Of Derivative}\\ \end{align}$$

  • Proof of Constant Property

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} c &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{c - c}{h}\right] \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{0}{h}\right] \\ &= 0 \end{align}$$

    Another way to think of this intuitevly is by seeing a derivative as the rate of change

    Since the constant function is always constant, it's graph is just a horizontal line at the same value

    This means it has no rate of change, so it's derivative is 0

  • Proof of Constant Coefficient Property

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} cf(x) &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{cf(x+h) - cf(x)}{h}\right] \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{c(f(x+h) - f(x))}{h}\right] &&\text{Factor out $c$}\\ &= c \lim_{h \to 0}\left[\frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\right] &&\text{Definition of Derivative}\\ &= cf'(x) &&\text{Limit Constant Property}\\ \end{align}$$

  • Proof of Power Rule

    Using Logarithmic Differentiation (which we haven't learned yet, but we will get to later)

    $$\begin{align} f(x) &= x^n \\ ln(f(x)) &= nln(x) &&\text{Using Log Exponent Property $ln(a^b) = bln(a)$} \\ \frac{d}{dx}ln(f(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} nln(x) \\ \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} &= \frac{n}{x} \\ f'(x) &= f(x)\frac{n}{x} \\ f'(x) &= \frac{nx^n}{x} \\ f'(x) &= nx^{n-1} &&\text{Using Exponent Division Property $\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}$}\\ \end{align}$$

Practice Problems
  • $\frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^2 + 4x - 1\right]$

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^2 + 4x - 1\right] \\ \frac{d}{dx} \left[2x^2\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[4x\right] + \frac{d}{dx} \left[1\right] \\ 2(2x) + 4(1) + 0 \\ 4x + 4 \end{align}$$

  • $\frac{d}{dx} x\sqrt{x}\sqrt[3]{x}$

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} x\sqrt{x}\sqrt[3]{x} \\ \frac{d}{dx} x \cdot x^{1/2} \cdot x^{1/3} \\ \frac{d}{dx} x^{11/6} &&\text{Using Exponent Product Property $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a + b }$}\\ \frac{11x^{5/6}}{6} &&\text{Using Power Rules}\\ \end{align}$$

  • $\frac{d}{dx} \frac{4}{3x}$

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} \frac{4}{3x} \\ \frac{4}{3} \frac{d}{dx} \frac{1}{x} &&\text{Using Constant Property}\\ \frac{4}{3} \frac{d}{dx} x^{-1} &&\text{Using Constant Property}\\ \frac{4}{3} -x^{-2} &&\text{Using Power Rule}\\ -\frac{4}{3x^2} &&\text{Simplify}\\ \end{align}$$

  • $\frac{d}{dx} \frac{x\pi \cdot e^3tan^{-1}{\sqrt{\pi}}}{\sqrt[5]{3^8}ln(4)sin(1)}$

    While this looks confusing, notice the only variable we have is the lone x and everything else is a constant

    $$\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx} \frac{x\pi \cdot e^3tan^{-1}{\sqrt{\pi}}}{\sqrt[5]{3^8}ln(4)sin(1)} \\ \frac{\pi \cdot e^3tan^{-1}{\sqrt{\pi}}}{\sqrt[5]{3^8}ln(4)sin(1)} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} x \\ \frac{\pi \cdot e^3tan^{-1}{\sqrt{\pi}}}{\sqrt[5]{3^8}ln(4)sin(1)} (1) \\ \frac{\pi \cdot e^3tan^{-1}{\sqrt{\pi}}}{\sqrt[5]{3^8}ln(4)sin(1)} \\ \end{align}$$
