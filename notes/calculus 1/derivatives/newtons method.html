Newton's method (or the Newtonâ€“Raphson method) is a practical use of derivatives to find roots/zeros of a function

First you have an initial guess $x_0$ that you believe is close to the root

For successive iterations

$$\begin{align} x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} \end{align}$$

The more you iterate, or in other words the more you do this process, the closer you get to the root

One of the uses for newton's method is for finding square roots, cube roots, or general n'th roots

How Does It Work?

If we create a tangent line at $(x_n,f(x_n))$, and find it's root (which is easier to do because it's a line)

This root will be closer to the actual root which is why we need to iterate it

The way we find the tangent line's root is by using the slope formula (which is equal to $f'(x_n)$) between $(x_{n+1},0)$ and $(x_n,f(x_n))$

$$\begin{align} f'(x_n) = \frac{f(x_n) - 0}{x_n - x_{n+1}} = \frac{f(x_n)}{x_n - x_{n+1}} \end{align}$$

If we solve this to get $x_{n+1}$

$$\begin{align} x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} \end{align}$$

The more we repeat this the closer we get to the actual root

the slope of the green line is $f'(x_n)$

It also goes without saying closer initial values will take less iterations

Examples

Approximate the positive root of $f(x) = 4x^{3}+2x^{2}-3x-1$

$f'(x) = 12x^{2}+4x-3$

Since we are trying to find the positive root, let's have an initial guess of $x_0 = .5$

$$\begin{align} x_0 &= .5 \\ x_1 &= .5 - \frac{f(.5)}{f'(.5)} = 1.25 \\ x_2 &= 1.25- \frac{f(.5)}{f'(.5)} \approx .9519 \\ x_3 &= .9519- \frac{f(.9519)}{f'(.9519)} \approx .8314 \\ x_4 &= .8314- \frac{f(.8314)}{f'(.8314)} \approx .8097 \\ x_5 &= .8097- \frac{f(.8097)}{f'(.8097)} \approx .8090 \\ x_6 &= .8090- \frac{f(.8090)}{f'(.8090)} \approx .8090 \\ \end{align}$$

We appear to be approaching a final value so we can say our root is approximately $.809$


Let's approximate the value of $\sqrt{2}$

What function has a root that is $\sqrt{2}$?

The positive root of $x^2 - 2$ works

$f(x) = x^2 - 2$

$f'(x) = 2x$

For the initial guess, I know that $1 < 2 < 4$, which leads to $\sqrt{1} < \sqrt{2} < \sqrt{4} \rightarrow 1 < \sqrt{2} < 2$

Since it's in between $[1,2]$, let's just choose 1.5

$$\begin{align} x_0 &= 1.5 \\ x_1 &= 1.5 - \frac{f(1.5)}{f'(1.5)} = 1.416 \\ x_2 &= 1.416- \frac{f( 1.416)}{f'( 1.416)} \approx 1.4142 \\ x_3 &= 1.4142- \frac{f(1.4142)}{f'(1.4142)} \approx 1.4142 \\ x_4 &= 1.4142- \frac{f(1.4142)}{f'(1.4142)} \approx 1.4142 \\ \end{align}$$

This starts approaching $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4142$

Possible Failure

Sometimes, newton's method won't work or give you another root that you weren't intending for

One possible cause is $f'(x_n) = 0$, which you can't divide by

Another possible issue is that the location of the zero is so steep, poor initial guesses causes the guesses to blow up (ex. $f(x) = \tan^{-1}(x), x_0 = 1.5)$

The guesses can also oscillate between two roots and not settle between one
