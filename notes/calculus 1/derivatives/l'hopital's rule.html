L'Hôpital's rule (pronounced loh-pee-tawl) is a theorem that makes solving some limits easier

L'Hôpital's rule states

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}\\ &\text{where} \\ f(c) = &g(c) = 0\\ &\text{or} \\ f(c) = &g(c) = \pm \infty\\ \end{align}$$

L'Hopital's rule (I'm going to not write the arrow over the o anymore) is commonly used to solve the following limits

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} &= \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} &= \pm \frac{\infty}{\infty} \end{align}$$

There are also times when you need to apply L'Hopital's rule multiple times

Examples

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{e^x - 1}{x} = \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

We have to apply L'Hopital's rule

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{e^x - 1}{x} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} e^x - 1}{\frac{d}{dx} x} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{e^x}{1} \\ &= 1 \end{align}$$


$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{x - xcos(x)}{x - \sin(x)} = \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{x - xcos(x)}{x - \sin(x)} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx}x - xcos(x)}{ \frac{d}{dx} x - \sin(x)} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{1 - \cos(x) + x\sin(x)}{1 - \cos(x)} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 0}x\sin(x) \\ &= 0 \\ \end{align}$$

Practice Problems
  • $\lim_{x \to 0}\frac{2e^{5x} - 25x^2 - 10x - 2}{5x^3}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{2e^{5x} - 25x^2 - 10x - 2}{5x^3} &= \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} 2e^{5x} - 25x^2 - 10x - 2}{\frac{d}{dx} 5x^3} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{10e^{5x} - 50x - 10}{15x^2} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{10e^{5x} - 50x - 10}{15x^2} &= \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} 10e^{5x} - 50x - 10}{\frac{d}{dx} 15x^2} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{50e^{5x} - 50}{30x} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{50e^{5x} - 50}{30x} &= \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} 50e^{5x} - 50}{\frac{d}{dx} 30x} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{250e^{5x}}{30} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{250e^{5x}}{30}& = \frac{250}{30} = \frac{25}{3} \end{align}$$

  • $\lim_{x \to 0}\left(\frac{1}{\sin(x)} - \frac{1}{x}\right)$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\left(\frac{1}{\sin(x)} - \frac{1}{x}\right) &= \infty - \infty \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\left(\frac{1}{\sin(x)} - \frac{1}{x}\right) &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{x- \sin(x)}{x\sin(x)} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} x- \sin(x)}{\frac{d}{dx} x\sin(x)} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{1 - \cos(x)}{\sin(x) + x\cos(x)} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{1 - \cos(x)}{\sin(x) + xcos(x)} &= \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} 1 - \cos(x)}{\frac{d}{dx} \sin(x) + xcos(x)} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x) + \cos(x) - xsin(x)} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x) + \cos(x) - x\sin(x)} &= \frac{0}{1 + 1 + 0} = 0\\ \end{align}$$

  • $\lim_{x \to 0}xln(x)$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}xln(x) &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{ln(x)}{1/x} = \frac{0}{0} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\frac{d}{dx} ln(x)}{\frac{d}{dx} 1/x} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{1/x}{-1/x^2} \\ \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{1/x}{-1/x^2} &= -\lim_{x \to 0}\frac{x^2}{x} = -\lim_{x \to 0}x \\ -\lim_{x \to 0}x &= 0 \end{align}$$

Proof

Case One: $f(c) = g(c) = 0$ and $g'(c) \neq 0$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x) - 0}{g(x) - 0} \\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x) - f(c)}{g(x) - g(c)} \\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{\frac{f(x) - f(c)}{x-c}}{\frac{g(x) - g(c)}{x-c}} \\ &= \frac{\lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x) - f(c)}{x-c}}{\lim_{x \to c}\frac{g(x) - g(c)}{x-c}} \\ &= \frac{f'(c)}{g'(c)} \end{align}$$

Case Two:$f(c) = g(c) = \infty$

if only the denominator is infinity, then the limit is easy to solve and we don't need to use L'hopital rule

First define $L$

$$\begin{align} L = \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \end{align}$$

Now we can rewrite the limit

$$\begin{align} L &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{1/g(x)}{1/f(x)} \\ \end{align}$$

Here both the numerator and denominator are $0$ so we can apply case one

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c}\frac{1/g(x)}{1/f(x)} &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{(1/g(x))'}{(1/f(x))'}\\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{-g'(x)/g(x)^2}{-f'(x)/f(x)^2}\\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)^2g'(x)}{g(x)^2f'(x)}\\ &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f(x)^2}{g(x)^2} \cdot \lim_{x \to c}\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}\\ L &= L^2 \cdot \lim_{x \to c}\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}\\ L^{-1} &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}\\ L &= \frac{1}{\lim_{x \to c}\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}}\\ L &= \frac{\lim_{x \to c} 1}{\lim_{x \to c}\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}}\\ L &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{ 1}{\frac{g'(x)}{f'(x)}}\\ L &= \lim_{x \to c}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}\\ \end{align}$$
