This note is about techniques using trig properties and u-sub to solve integrals in the form

$$\begin{align} \int{\sin^m(x)\cos^n(x) \ dx} \\ \int{\sec^m(x)\tan^n(x) \ dx} \\ \int{\csc^m(x)\cot^n(x) \ dx} \\ \end{align}$$

Where $m$ and $n$ integers

$\sin^m(x)\cos^n(x)$

Here there are 4 cases for $m$ and $n$

  1. $m$ (Power of $\sin$) is odd
    1. Factor out 1 $\sin$ to make the power even
    2. Factor out larger power of sine into the form of $(\sin^2)^k$
    3. Use $\sin^2 + \cos^2 = 1 \rightarrow \sin^2 = 1 - \cos^2$ to make the sine into cosine
    4. Use a u-sub of $u = \cos(x)$ and $du = -\sin(x) \ dx$

  2. $n$ (Power of $\cos$) is odd
    1. Factor out 1 $\cos$ to make the power even
    2. Factor out larger power of cosine into the form of $(\cos^2)^k$
    3. Use $\sin^2 + \cos^2 = 1 \rightarrow \cos^2 = 1 - \sin^2$ to make the cosine into sine
    4. Use a u-sub of $u = \sin(x)$ and $du = \cos(x) \ dx$

  3. Both $m$ and $n$ are odd (use either 1 or 2)

  4. Both $m$ and $n$ are even
    1. Use either $\sin^2(x) = \frac{1}{2}(1 - \cos(2x))$ and/or $\cos^2(x) = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \cos(2x))$

$\sec^m(x)\tan^n(x)$

Here there are once again 4 cases for $m$ and $n$

  1. $m$ (Power of $\sec$) is even
    1. Factor out 2 $\sec$ to make a seperate $\sec^2$ term
    2. Factor out larger power of secant into the form of $(\sec^2)^k$
    3. Use $\tan^2 + 1 = sec^2(x)$ to make the secant into tangent
    4. Use a u-sub of $u = \tan(x)$ and $du = \sec^2(x) \ dx$

  2. $n$ (Power of $\tan$) is odd
    1. Factor out 1 $\tan$ and 1 $\sec$ to make the power of tangent even
    2. Factor out power of tangent into the form of $(\tan^2)^k$
    3. Use $\tan^2 + 1 = sec^2(x) \rightarrow \tan^2 = \sec^2 - 1$ to make the cosine into sine
    4. Use a u-sub of $u = \sec(x)$ and $du = \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx$

  3. If $m$ is even or $n$ is odd (use either 1 or 2)

  4. $m$ is odd and $n$ is even
    1. Utilize the integral $\int{\sec(x) \ dx} = \ln|\sec(x) + \tan(x)| + C$

Similar methods can be used for $\csc^m(x)\cot^n(x)$

Examples

$$\begin{align} &\int{\sin^6(x)\cos^3(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sin^6(x)\cos^2(x)\cos(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sin^6(x)(1 - sin^2(x))\cos(x) \ dx} \\ &u = \sin(x), du = \cos(x) \ dx \\ &\int{u^6(1 - u^2) \ du} \\ &\int{u^6 - u^{8} \ du} \\ &\frac{u^7}{7} - \frac{u^{9}}{9} \\ &\frac{\sin^7(x)}{7} - \frac{\sin^{9}(x)}{9} + C \\ \end{align}$$


$$\begin{align} &\int{\sec^5(x)\tan^3(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sec^4(x)\tan^2(x) \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(\sec^2(x))^2\tan^2(x) \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(\tan^2(x) + 1)^2\tan^2(x) \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &u = \tan(x), du = \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx \\ &\int{(u^2 + 1)^2 u^2 \ du} \\ &\int{(u^4 + 2u^2 + 1)u^2 \ du} \\ &\int{u^6 + 2u^4 + u^2 \ du} \\ &\frac{u^7}{7} + \frac{2u^5}{5} + \frac{u^3}{3} \\ &\frac{\tan(x)^7}{7} + \frac{2\tan(x)^5}{5} + \frac{\tan(x)^3}{3} + C\\ \end{align}$$


$$\begin{align} &\int{\cos^5(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\cos^4(x)\cos(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(\cos^2(x))^2\cos(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(1-\sin^2(x))^2\cos(x) \ dx} \\ &u = \sin(x), du = \cos(x) \ dx \\ &\int{(1-u^2)^2 \ du} \\ &\int{1-2u^2+u^4 \ du} \\ &u-\frac{2u^3}{3}+\frac{u^5}{5} \\ &\sin(x)-\frac{2\sin(x)^3}{3}+\frac{\sin(x)^5}{5} + C \\ \end{align}$$

Practice Problems
  • $\int{\sin^5(x) \ dx}$

    $$\begin{align} &\int{\sin^5(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sin^4(x)\sin(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(\sin^2(x))^2\sin(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(1 - \cos^2(x))^2\sin(x) \ dx} \\ &u = \cos(x), du = -\sin(x) \ dx \\ &-\int{(1 - u^2)^2 \ du} \\ &-\int{1 -2u^2 + u^4 \ du} \\ &-\left(u -\frac{2u^3}{3} + \frac{u^5}{5} \right) \\ &-\cos(x) + \frac{2\cos(x)^3}{3} - \frac{\cos(x)^5}{5} + C\\ \end{align}$$

  • $\int{\sin^4(x) \ dx}$

    $$\begin{align} &\int{\sin^4(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{(\sin^2(x))^2 \ dx} \\ &\int{\left(\frac{1}{2}\left(1 - \cos(2x)\right)\right)^2 \ dx} \\ &\int{\frac{\left(1 - \cos(2x)\right)^2}{4} \ dx} \\ &\frac{1}{4}\int{\left(1 - \cos(2x)\right)^2 \ dx} \\ &\frac{1}{4}\int{1 - 2\cos(2x) + cos^2(2x)\ dx} \\ &\frac{1}{4}\int{1 - 2\cos(2x) + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\cos(2x)}{2} \ dx} \\ &\frac{1}{4}\int{\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3\cos(2x)}{2} \ dx} \\ &\frac{1}{4}\int{\frac{3}{2}\left(1 - \cos(2x)\right) \ dx} \\ &\frac{3}{8}\int{1 - \cos(2x) \ dx} \\ &\frac{3}{8}\left(x - \frac{\sin(2x)}{2}\right) \\ & \frac{3x}{8}- \frac{3\sin(2x)}{16} + C \\ \end{align}$$

  • $\int{\sec^9(x)\tan^5(x) \ dx}$

    $$\begin{align} &\int{\sec^9(x)\tan^5(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sec^8(x)\tan^4(x)\sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sec^8(x)(\tan^2(x))^2\sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &\int{\sec^8(x)(\sec^2(x) - 1)^2\sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx} \\ &u = \sec(x), du = \sec(x)\tan(x) \ dx \\ &\int{u^8(u^2 - 1)^2 \ du} \\ &\int{u^8(u^4 - 2u^2 + 1) \ du} \\ &\int{u^{12} - 2u^{10} + u^8 \ du} \\ &\frac{u^{13}}{13} - \frac{2u^{11}}{11} + \frac{u^9}{9} \\ &\frac{\sec(x)^{13}}{13} - \frac{2\sec(x)^{11}}{11} + \frac{\sec(x)^9}{9} + C \\ \end{align}$$
