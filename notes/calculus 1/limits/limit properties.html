When solving limits, it's easier to split the limits up and solve each indivually

This is what limit properties achieve

Assume that $\lim_\limits{x \to c} f(x)$ and $\lim_\limits{x \to c} g(x)$ exist

Sum/Difference Property:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c} \left[f(x) \pm g(x)\right] = \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \pm \lim_{x \to c} g(x) \end{align}$$

Constant Coefficient Property:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c} \left[cf(x)\right] = c \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \end{align}$$

Product Property:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c} \left[f(x) \cdot g(x)\right] = \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to c} g(x) \end{align}$$

Quotient Property:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim_\limits{x \to c} f(x)}{\lim_\limits{x \to c} g(x)} \end{align}$$

Composition Property:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to c} f(g(x)) = f(\lim_{x \to c}g(x)) &&\text{If $f(x)$ is continous at $x=L$ where $\lim_{x \to c}g(x)=L$} \end{align}$$

The composition property also means you switch the $f(x)$ and the limit
