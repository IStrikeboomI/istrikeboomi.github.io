The main idea behing limits is approaching a point on a graph for a function

The notation for a limit is:

$$\lim_{x \to c}f(x) = n$$

Here $f(x)$ is the function that we are approaching

$x$ is the variable for the function

$c$ is some constant value that we are approaching

$n$ is the answer for the limit

Let's solve some limits for the graph above

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to -1}f(x) &= 1 \tag{1} \\ \lim_{x \to 1}f(x) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \to 2}f(x) &= 2 \end{align}$$

The way you would say a limit like (1) is "As $x$ approaches $-1$, $f(x)$ approaches $1$"

While this just seems like plugging in numbers for $f(x)$, it's not about the value of $f(x)$ it's about what it approaches

Here:

$$f(2) = -3$$

But the limit:

$$\lim_{x \to 2}f(x) = 2$$

Sometimes, the limit might not exist

Here:

$$f(-1) = 0$$

But the limit:

$$\lim_{x \to -1}f(x) = DNE$$

The limit is DNE, which mean "Does Not Exist"

The limit doesn't exist because on the left side the limit approaches 1, but the right side approaches -1

A limit is DNE if there is nothing to approach, or it is different both on sides

For Example:

Limits:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to -3} f(x) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \to -2} f(x) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \to -1} f(x) &= DNE \\ \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) &= DNE \\ \lim_{x \to 1} f(x) &= 1 \\ \end{align}$$

How Do We Solve Limits?

For many limits, you don't have a graph provided, just a function

The most basic method is direct substitution, which is plugging in the x value

For Example:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{cos(\pi x)}{x} &= \frac{cos(\pi(1))}{(1)} \\ &= cos(\pi) \\ &= -1 \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{h \to -1}\frac{h^3 - 4h^2 + h - 6}{h - 5} &= \frac{(-1)^3 - 4(-1)^2 + (-1) - 6}{(-1) - 5}\\ &= \frac{-1 - 4(1) -1 - 6}{-1 - 5}\\ &= \frac{-12}{-6}\\ &= 2 \end{align}$$

Note: direct substituion will NOT work if it's one of the indeterminate forms, most commonly $\frac{0}{0}$

For Example:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{sin(x)}{x} &= \frac{sin(0)}{0}\\ &= \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

So, direct substituion will NOT work for the limit above

In order to solve it you will need to use other methods (which we will learn later), or look at the graph

Practice Problems
  • $ \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{4 - x}{x + 1} $

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{4 - x}{x + 1} &= \frac{4 - 1}{1 + 1}\\ &= \frac{3}{2} \end{align}$$

  • $ \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 3} $

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 3} &= \frac{3^2 - 5(3) + 6}{3 - 3}\\ &= \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

    Since we got $\frac{0}{0}$ we can't use direct substituion

    We can instead look at the graph

    We can see that when x approaches 3, the function approaches 1

  • $ \lim_{t \to e}ln(\sqrt{t}) $

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{t \to e}ln(\sqrt{t}) &= ln(\sqrt{e})\\ &= \frac{1}{2}ln(e) &&\text{Using log property $ln(a^b) = bln(a)$}\\ &= \frac{1}{2} \end{align}$$
