We talked earlier about how limits that result in $\frac{0}{0}$ by direct substituion doesn't work

Solving limits algebraically is using algebra to simplyfy the limit to where direct substituion works

The main goal to prevent $\frac{0}{0}$ is to cancel out a term in the numerator and denominator that were going to 0

Imagine the limit:

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 3} &= \frac{3^2 - 5(3) + 6}{3 - 3}\\ &= \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

Direct substituion gives us \frac{0}{0}, so we need to simplyfy it so it can work

First technique is to factor

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 3} &= \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{(x - 3)(x-2)}{x - 3}\\ \end{align}$$

We can cancel the $(x - 3)$

Normally when we cancel, it creates a hole, but since we are dealing with limits and approaching, we don't need to care about the hole

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 3}\frac{(x - 3)(x-2)}{x - 3} &= \lim_{x \to 3}(x-2)\\ &= 3 - 2\\ &= 1 \end{align}$$

We get 1 and we can verify with the graph

Another technique, commonly involving square roots, is to multiply by the conjugate

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} &= \frac{\sqrt{4} - 2}{4 - 4} &= \frac{0}{0} \end{align}$$

With direct substituion, we get $\frac{0}{0}$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} &= \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2} \end{align}$$

We multiply by the conjugate, because it is essentially multiplying by 1, we just rewrite 1 as $\frac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2}$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} &= \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2} &= \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{(\sqrt{x})^2 - (2)^2}{(x - 4)(\sqrt{x} + 2)} &&\text{We can distribute the numerator easily using difference of squares} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{x - 4}{(x - 4)(\sqrt{x} + 2)} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 4}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x} + 2} \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{4} + 2} \\ &= \frac{1}{2 + 2} \\ &= \frac{1}{4} \\ \end{align}$$

Practice Problems
  • $ \lim_{x \to -1}\frac{x^3 + 1}{x + 1}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to -1}\frac{x^3 + 1}{x + 1} &= \lim_{x \to -1}\frac{(x+1)(x^2 - x + 1)}{x + 1} &&\text{Using difference of cubes}\\ &= \lim_{x \to -1}(x^2 - x + 1) \\ &= (-1)^2 - (-1) + 1 \\ &= 1 + 1 + 1 \\ &= 3 \end{align}$$

  • $ \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{(2 + x)^2 - (2 + x)}{x + 2}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{(2 + x)^2 - (2 + x)}{x + 2} &= \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{4 + 4x + x^2 - (2 + x)}{x + 2}\\ &= \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{2 + 3x + x^2}{x + 2}\\ &= \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{x^2 + 3x + 2}{x + 2} &&\text{Rearrange Terms}\\ &= \lim_{x \to -2} \frac{(x + 2)(x + 1)}{x + 2} &= \lim_{x \to -2} (x + 1) \\ &= -2 + 1 \\ &= -1 \end{align}$$

  • $ \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{x - 1}{x^2 + x - 2}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{x - 1}{x^2 + x - 2} &= \lim_{x \to 1}\frac{x - 1}{(x-1)(x+2)}\\ &= \lim_{x \to 1}(x+2)\\ &= 1 + 2\\ &= 3 \end{align}$$

  • $ \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{\frac{1}{t} - \frac{1}{4}}{t - 4}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{\frac{1}{t} - \frac{1}{4}}{t - 4} &= \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{\frac{4}{4t} - \frac{t}{4t}}{t - 4} &&\text{Make fractions on top match denominators}\\ &= \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{\frac{4 - t}{4t}}{t - 4} &&\text{Add fractions}\\ &= \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{4 - t}{4t(t - 4)} &&\text{Distribute fractions}\\ &= \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{-(t - 4)}{4t(t - 4)} &&\text{Factor out negative in numerator to cancel later}\\ &= \lim_{t \to 4}\frac{-1}{4t} &&\text{Cancel out terms}\\ &= \frac{-1}{4(4)} \\ &= -\frac{1}{16} \end{align}$$

  • $\lim_{k \to 5}\frac{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} - 6}{k - 5}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} - 6}{k - 5} &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} - 6}{k - 5} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6}{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6} \\ &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{(\sqrt{k^2 + 11})^2 - 6^2}{(k - 5)(\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6)} \\ &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{k^2 + 11 - 36}{(k - 5)(\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6)} \\ &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{k^2 - 25}{(k - 5)(\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6)} \\ &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{(k - 5)(k + 5)}{(k - 5)(\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6)} \\ &= \lim_{k \to 5}\frac{k + 5}{\sqrt{k^2 + 11} + 6} \\ &= \frac{5 + 5}{\sqrt{5^2 + 11} + 6} \\ &= \frac{10}{\sqrt{36} + 6} \\ &= \frac{10}{6 + 6} \\ &= \frac{10}{12} \\ &= \frac{5}{6} \\ \end{align}$$
