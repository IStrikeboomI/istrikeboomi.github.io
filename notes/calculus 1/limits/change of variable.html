Change of variable is a technique to solve limits with strange variables

We change the variable and the value approaching to simplify the limit

$$\begin{align} \text{If } \lim_{x \to a}g(x) = b \text{, then} \lim_{x \to a}f(g(x)) = \lim_{y \to b}f(y) \end{align}$$

Normally, the new variable that we replace with is $u$ (but it can be anything) and it is in terms of $x$

When we change the variable, all the $x$ have to be changed in terms of $u$

To find the new approach value, plug the value $x$ approaches into $u$

Examples

$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{(x + 8)^{1/3} - 2}{x} \end{align}$$

If we tried to use direct substituion, we would get $\frac{0}{0}$

Instead I'll substitute $u = (x + 8)^{1/3}$

We need to take care of the $x$ in the denominaotor, but we can write $x$ in terms of $u$ as $x = u^3 - 8$

For the new approach value, plug $x \to 0$ into $u$, $u = (0 + 8)^{1/3} = 2$, which mean $u \to 2$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{u \to 2}\frac{u - 2}{u^3 - 8} &= \lim_{u \to 2}\frac{u - 2}{(u-2)(u^2 + 2u + 4)} &&\text{Difference Of Cubes} \\ &= \lim_{u \to 2}\frac{1}{u^2 + 2u + 4} \\ &= \frac{1}{(2)^2 + 2(2) + 4} \\ &= \frac{1}{12} \\ \end{align}$$


$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to \infty}&\frac{\sin\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)}{\frac{1}{x}} \\ &\theta = \frac{1}{x} \\ \text{as } x \to \infty&, \theta \to 0 \\ \lim_{\theta \to 0}&\frac{\sin\left(\theta\right)}{\theta} = 1\\ \end{align}$$

The limit above is 1 because it was covered in the previous note

Practice Problems
  • $\lim_\limits{x \to 3}\frac{\cos(x-3) - 1}{x - 3}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_\limits{x \to 3}\frac{\cos(x-3) - 1}{x - 3} \\ u = x - 3 \\ x \to 3, u \to 0 \\ \lim_\limits{u \to 0}\frac{\cos(u) - 1}{u} = 0 \end{align}$$

    The above limit is the corollary from the previous note

    $\lim_\limits{x \to 1}\frac{\sqrt[3]{x} - 1}{x - 1}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_\limits{x \to 1}\frac{\sqrt[3]{x} - 1}{x - 1} \\ u = \sqrt[3]{x} \to u^3 = x \\ x \to 1, u \to 1 \\ \lim_\limits{u \to 1}\frac{u - 1}{u^3 - 1} \\ \lim_\limits{u \to 1}\frac{u - 1}{(u - 1)(u^2 + 2u + 1)} &&\text{Difference Of Cubes} \\ \lim_\limits{u \to 1}\frac{1}{u^2 + 2u + 1} = \frac{1}{4}\\ \end{align}$$

    $\lim_\limits{h \to 0^+}(1 + h)^{1/h}$

    $$\begin{align} \lim_\limits{h \to 0^+}(1 + h)^{1/h} \\ h = \frac{1}{n} \to \frac{1}{h} = n \\ h \to 0^+,n \to \infty \\ \lim_\limits{n \to \infty}\left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right)^{n} = e &&\text{By Definition Of $e$} \end{align}$$
