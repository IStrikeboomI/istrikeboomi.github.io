So far we have only looked at binary numbers that are whole numbers.

Now it's time to look at fractional binary numbers.

Note: This is how to store binary fractional numbers in the math world

We will be looking at how fractional binary numbers are stored in computers later (but it's similar)


Fractional Binary numbers are stored by having digits left of the binary point

The binary point is the equivalent of the decimal point in base 10

Have an index of $-1,-2,-3,...$, you use the same formula

So the indicies of $101.101_2$ are $2^2, 2^1, 2^0, 2^{-1}, 2^{-2}, 2^{-3}$

Converting Fractional Binary To Decimal

To convert fractional binary to decimal

You use the same formula as normal binary, but with negative powers of 2

You can do this by converting the whole and fractional parts separately

For example, to convert the binary number $101.101_2$ to decimal:

First, convert the whole part $101_2$: 5

Then, convert the fractional part $0.101_2$: $2^{-1} + 2^{-3} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{8} = \frac{5}{8}$

So, $101.101_2 = 5 + \frac{5}{8} = 5.625$

Another way to do it is to use the formula directly:

$$\begin{align} 1 \cdot 2^2 + 0 \cdot 2^1 + 1 \cdot 2^0 + 1 \cdot 2^{-1} + 0 \cdot 2^{-2} + 1 \cdot 2^{-3} = 4 + 0 + 1 + 0.5 + 0 + 0.125 = 5.625 \end{align}$$


Another example, convert $.00110101_2$ to decimal:

First, convert the whole part $0_2$: 0

Then, convert the fractional part $.00110101_2$: $2^{-3} + 2^{-4} + 2^{-6} + 2^{-8} = \frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{16} + \frac{1}{64} + \frac{1}{256}$

So, $.00110101_2 = \frac{32 + 16 + 4 + 1}{256} = \frac{53}{256} = 0.2060546875$

Converting Decimal To Fractional Binary

To convert decimal to fractional binary, you can convert the whole and fractional parts separately

For example, to convert $6.625$ to binary:

First, convert the whole part $6$ to binary: $110_2$

Then, convert the fractional part $0.625$ to binary:

The way to do this is keep multiplying the fractional part by 2:

You then take the whole part (either 0 or 1) and append it to the left of the binary point:

Repeat this process until the fractional part becomes 0:

$0.625 \times 2 = 1.25$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.25$

$0.25 \times 2 = 0.5$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.5$

$0.5 \times 2 = 1.0$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0$

So, the binary representation of $0.625$ is $.101_2$

Combining both parts, we get $6.625 = 110.101_2$


Another example, convert $0.2$ to binary:

Since the whole part is $0$, we only need to convert the fractional part $0.2$ to binary:

$0.2 \times 2 = 0.4$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.4$

$0.4 \times 2 = 0.8$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.8$

$0.8 \times 2 = 1.6$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.6$

$0.6 \times 2 = 1.2$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.2$

$0.2 \times 2 = 0.4$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.4$

Notice that we are back to $0.4$, so the pattern will repeat

So, the binary representation of $0.2$ is approximately $0.0011\overline{0011}_2$

This means that $0.2$ cannot be represented exactly in binary

This is similar to how you cannot represent $1/7$ exactly in decimal (it's $0.142857\overline{142857}$)


Another example, convert $.7578125$ to binary:

Since the whole part is $0$, we only need to convert the fractional part $0.7578125$ to binary:

$0.7578125 \times 2 = 1.515625$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.515625$

$0.515625 \times 2 = 1.03125$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.03125$

$0.03125 \times 2 = 0.0625$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.0625$

$0.0625 \times 2 = 0.125$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.125$

$0.125 \times 2 = 0.25$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.25$

$0.25 \times 2 = 0.5$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.5$

$0.5 \times 2 = 1.0$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0$

So, the binary representation of $0.7578125$ is $.1100001_2$

Arithmetic

Fractional binary arithmetic works the same way as normal binary arithmetic

Just make sure to line up the binary points when adding

For example, to add $101.101_2$ ($5.625$) and $10.11_2$ ($2.75$):

$$\begin{align} 101&.101 \\ + \ \ 10&.110 \\ \hline 1000&.011 \\ \end{align}$$

The result is $1000.011_2$ ($8.375$)


For multiplying, it's a little different

Another example, to multiply $11.01_2$ ($3.25$) and $10.1_2$ ($2.5$):

To multiply drop the binary point for the numbers

Then at the end, place the binary point back in the result

You place it by how many fractional points you removed combined

So we multiply $1101_2$ and $101_2$ (we dropped 3 fractional points)

$$\begin{align} 1101 \\ \times 101 \\ \hline 1101\\ 0000 \ \ \\ + 1101 \ \ \ \ \\ \hline 1000001 \end{align}$$

Now we place the binary point back in, counting 3 places from the right

The result is $1000.001_2$ ($8.125$)

Practice Problems
  • Convert $1110.010101110_2$ to decimal

    First, convert the whole part $1110_2$: 14

    Then, convert the fractional part $0.010101110_2$:

    $2^{-2} + 2^{-4} + 2^{-6} + 2^{-7} + 2^{-8} = .33984375$

    So, $1110.010101110_2 = 14 + .33984375 = 14.33984375$

  • Convert $11.66015625$ to binary

    First, convert the whole part $11$ to binary: $1011_2$

    Then, convert the fractional part $0.66015625$ to binary:

    $0.66015625 \times 2 = 1.3203125$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.3203125$

    $0.3203125 \times 2 = 0.640625$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.640625$

    $0.640625 \times 2 = 1.28125$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.28125$

    $0.28125 \times 2 = 0.5625$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.5625$

    $0.5625 \times 2 = 1.125$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0.125$

    $0.125 \times 2 = 0.25$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.25$

    $0.25 \times 2 = 0.5$ → integer part is $0$, fractional part is $0.5$

    $0.5 \times 2 = 1.0$ → integer part is $1$, fractional part is $0$

    So, the binary representation of $0.66015625$ is $.10101001_2$

    Combining both parts, we get $11.66015625 = 1011.10101001_2$

  • Add $6.4375$ and $12.9765625$ in binary

    First, convert both numbers to binary:

    $6.4375 = 110.0111_2$

    $12.9765625 = 1100.1111101_2$

    Now, add the two binary numbers, making sure to line up the binary points:

    $$\begin{align} \ \ 110&.0111000 \\ + 1100&.1111101 \\ \hline 10011&.0110101 \\ \end{align}$$

    The result is $10011.0110101_2$ ($19.4140625$)

  • Multiply $2.75$ and $1.125$ in binary

    First, convert both numbers to binary:

    $2.75 = 10.11_2$

    $1.125 = 1.001_2$

    Now, multiply the two binary numbers:

    We drop 2 binary points for the first number and 3 from the second number

    $$\begin{align} 1011 \\ \times 1001 \\ \hline 1011\\ 0000 \ \ \\ 0000 \ \ \ \ \\ + 1011 \ \ \ \ \ \ \\ \hline 1100011 \end{align}$$

    Now we place the binary point back in, counting 5 places from the right

    The result is $11.00011_2$ ($3.09375$)
