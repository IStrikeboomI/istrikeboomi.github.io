In the positional numeral system (which is what we typically use for numbers),

The base, or the radix is the number of digits there are to use

Each digit in the number contributes $\text{digit} \cdot \text{radix}^\text{index}$

The sum of contributions gives the full number

The way a number is denoted in another base is the number with a subscript of the base in base-10


The most common system is base-10, or decimal, which is what we use for regular numbers

Computers use base-2, or binary to store information

Computer information can also be read in base-16 or hexademical


The Positional Numeral System

What does the number $2149$ mean?

It means a 1000 2s plus a 100 1s plus 10 4s and 1 9

It other words

$$\begin{align} 2149 &= 2000 + 100 + 40 + 9 \\ &= 2 \cdot 10^3 + 1 \cdot 10^2 + 4 \cdot 10^1 + 9 \cdot 10^0 \end{align}$$

Notice how each digit of the number has an index (that starts at 0) which is the exponent

The 2 is at index 3

The 1 is at index 2

The 4 is at index 1

The 9 is at index 0

This was in base-10 (decimal) because there are 10 possible digits (0 through 9)

Let's try to express the quantity 2419, but we only have 3 digits (0,1,2), so base-3

When we express numbers we use a subscript to denote the base

$$\begin{align} 2149_{10} = 2221121_3 &= 2_3 \cdot 3_{10}^6 + 2_3 \cdot 3_{10}^5 + 2_3 \cdot 3_{10}^4 + 1_3 \cdot 3_{10}^3 + 1_3 \cdot 3_{10}^2 + 2_3 \cdot 3_{10}^1 + 1_3 \cdot 3_{10}^0 \\ &=1458_{10} + 486_{10} + 162_{10} + 27_{10} + 9_{10} + 6_{10} + 2_{10} \\ &= 2149_{10} \end{align}$$

For simplicity, the rest of numbers I will write in base-10/decimal won't have a subscript

Here's a final color coded example showing how each part of the number corresponds

$$\begin{align} &\textcolor{red}{1542}_{\textcolor{green}{6}}\\ \text{Index Position:} &\textcolor{blue}{3210} \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} 1542_6 = \textcolor{red}{1} \cdot \textcolor{green}{6}^\textcolor{blue}{3} + \textcolor{red}{5} \cdot \textcolor{green}{6}^\textcolor{blue}{2} + \textcolor{red}{1} \cdot \textcolor{green}{4}^\textcolor{blue}{1} + \textcolor{red}{2} \cdot \textcolor{green}{6}^\textcolor{blue}{0} = 422_{10} \end{align}$$

Bases Larger than 10

Since we only have 10 common digits (0 through 9), what do we use after base-10?

We use the alphabet! Since we can also use lower and uppercase, this gives us 46 extra characters

Combining this with earlier gives us a possible base-56

You can go past the alphabet and use symbols, but theres not a commonplace system for that

One of the most common uses of the alphbet in numbers is base-16 (hexademical)

Hexadecimal uses 0 through 9 and then a through f

a represents 11, b represents 12, c represents 13, d represents 14, and f represents 15

There's not a common standard wheter or not to use lowercase or uppercase numbers, use either

For example, here are some valid base-16 numbers:

$$\begin{align} 2B41_{16} &= 9393 \\ 100202_{16} &= 1049090\\ DEAD_{16} &= 57005 \\ 2_{16} &= 2 \\ \end{align}$$

Conversion With Decimal

To convert from base-10 to base-n

Divide the number by n and keep the result and remainder

Repeat this process with the result until it's 0

Write the final number from left to right using the remainders from bottom to top


To convert a number in base-n to base-10

Use the formula $\text{digit} \cdot \text{radix}^\text{index}$ and add up the result from each digit


Example: Convert $642$ to base-4 and back to base-10

$$\begin{align} 642 / 4 &= 163 \ R \ 0 \\ 163 / 4 &= 40 \ R \ 3 \\ 40 / 4 &= 10 \ R \ 0 \\ 10 / 4 &= 2 \ R \ 2 \\ 2 / 4 &= 0 \ R \ 2 \\ \end{align}$$

Putting this together we get $22030_4$

To convert this back to base-10

$$\begin{align} 2 \cdot 4^4 + 2 \cdot 4^3 + 0 \cdot 4^2 + 3 \cdot 4^1 + 0 \cdot 4^0 = 512 + 128 + 12 = 652 \end{align}$$

We get back our original number

Counting

How do we count in base-10? We start at 0 and increment all the way until the last digit 9

When we get to 9, there's not another digit so we reset it to 0 and increment it onto the next digit

For example:

$$\begin{align} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ \vdots \\ 9 \\ 10 \\ 11 \\ \vdots \\ 19 \\ 20 \\ \vdots \end{align}$$

This same principle applies to any number base system

For example, counting in base-2

$$\begin{align} 0_2,1_2,10_2,11_2,100_2,101_2,110_2,111_2 \end{align}$$

Base-3

$$\begin{align} 0_3,1_3,2_3,10_3,11_3,12_3,20_3,\ldots \end{align}$$

This is also why we need to denote the number base we are using because the number n in base-n will always be 10

10 in base-2 is 2, 10 in base-10 is 10, 10 in base-16 is 16, and so on and so forth

Practice Problems
  • Convert $531_{12}$ to base-10

    $$\begin{align} 5 \cdot 12^2 + 3 \cdot 12^1 + 1 \cdot 12^0 = 720 + 36 + 1 = 757 \end{align}$$

  • Convert $53_{10}$ to base-20

    $$\begin{align} 52 / 20 = 2 \ R \ 13 \\ 2 / 20 = 0 \ R \ 2 \\ \end{align}$$

    The remainder of 13 corresponds to $D$

    $53_{10} = 2D_{20}$

  • Convert $243_{7}$ to base-12 (hint: convert to base-10 first)

    $$\begin{align} 243_{7} = 2 \cdot 7^2 + 4 \cdot 7^1 + 3 \cdot 7^0 = 98 + 28 + 3 = 129_{10} \end{align}$$

    $$\begin{align} 129 / 12 = 10 \ R \ 9 \\ 10 / 12 = 0 \ R \ 10 \\ \end{align}$$

    So $243_7 = A9_{12}$
