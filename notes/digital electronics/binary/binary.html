Binary (or base-2) is the primary number system of computers

It is made up of only 1s and 0s, this is good because we can express it as ons and offs

Binary numbers are denoted by a subscript of 2 or they start with 0b

Conversion Between Decimal And Binary

The following here is how to convert an unsigned integer (positive, whole number) to binary

To convert from decimal (the system we normally use) to binary, you need to keep dividing by 2

Divide the number by 2 and store and the result and the remainder

Repeat this process using the result until you get 0

Write the final number from left to right using the remainders from bottom to top


To convert from binary to decimal, add up each of the terms that have a 1 using the formula $2^\text{index}$

Don't forget that index starts at zero

Example: convert 43 to binary and convert it back

$$\begin{align} 43 / 2 &= 21 \ R \ 1 \\ 21 / 2 &= 10 \ R \ 1 \\ 10 / 2 &= 5 \ R \ 0 \\ 5 / 2 &= 2 \ R \ 1 \\ 2 / 2 &= 1 \ R \ 0 \\ 1 / 2 &= 0 \ R \ 1 \\ \\ 43_{10} &= 101011_{2} \text{ or } 0b101011 \\ 2^0 + 2^1 + 2^3 + 2^5 &= 1 + 2 + 8 + 32 = 43_{10} \end{align}$$

Counting In Binary

Counting in binary is a useful skill for making truth tables later on and other circuits

The way we count is simple, first start at the first digit

If it's 0 set it to 1 and we are done

If it's 1 set it to 0 and move onto the next digit and repeat previous step

Here is 0 to 16 in binary

$$\begin{align} 0_2 &= 0_{10} \\ 1_2 &= 1_{10} \\ 10_2 &= 2_{10} \\ 11_2 &= 3_{10} \\ 100_2 &= 4_{10} \\ 101_2 &= 5_{10} \\ 110_2 &= 6_{10} \\ 111_2 &= 7_{10} \\ 1000_2 &= 8_{10} \\ 1001_2 &= 9_{10} \\ 1010_2 &= 10_{10} \\ 1011_2 &= 11_{10} \\ 1100_2 &= 12_{10} \\ 1101_2 &= 13_{10} \\ 1110_2 &= 14_{10} \\ 1111_2 &= 15_{10} \\ 10000_2 &= 16_{10} \\ \end{align}$$

Binary Tricks

if the first (right-most) digit is 1, the number is odd

Moving all the digits to the left by 1 is the same as multiplying by 2 (pad the right-most digit by 0)

Moving all the digits to the right by 1 is the same as dividing by 2 (rounds down)

The operator for moving digits left is called a left shift and is denoted by <<

The operator for moving digits right is called a right shift and is denoted by >>

If there is only a single 1 in the binary number then it's a power of 2 ($2^n$)

Practice Problems
  • Convert $125_{10}$ to binary

    $$\begin{align} 125 / 2 &= 62 \ R \ 1 \\ 62 / 2 &= 31 \ R \ 0 \\ 31 / 2 &= 15 \ R \ 1 \\ 15 / 2 &= 7 \ R \ 1 \\ 7 / 2 &= 3 \ R \ 1 \\ 3 / 2 &= 1 \ R \ 1 \\ 1 / 2 &= 0 \ R \ 1 \\ \\ 125_{10} &= 1111101_2 \end{align}$$

  • Convert $0b01011011$ to decimal

    $$\begin{align} 0b01011011 = 2^6 + 2^4 + 2^3 + 2^1 + 2^0 = 91_{10} \end{align}$$

  • Multiply $1001101_2$ by 4 (Convert to decimal to check your answer)

    Since multiplying by 4 is the same as multiplying 2 twice, so shift digits left by 2

    $$\begin{align} 1001101_2 \cdot 4_{10} = 100110100_2 \end{align}$$

    If we convert both each to decimal we can check

    $$\begin{align} 1001101_2 &= 2^6 + 2^3 + 2^2 + 2^0 = 77 \\ 100110100_2 &= 2^8 + 2^5 + 2^4 + 2^2 = 308 = 77 \cdot 4\\ \end{align}$$
