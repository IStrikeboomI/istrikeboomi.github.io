So far we have covered Ohm's law, KCL, KVL

To recap

  • Ohm's Law: $V = IR$
  • KCL: The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum of currents leaving a node
  • KVL: The sum of voltages around any closed loop in a circuit is zero

Now we will see how to use these laws to solve circuits

What we mean by "solving circuits" is finding currents and voltages at points in a circuit

Procedure

  1. Identify all the nodes and loops in the circuit
  2. Assign polarity to all resistors if they don't have one
  3. Create KCL and KVL equations (use Ohm's law to voltage of resistors)
  4. Set up System of Equations (Make sure each unkown is in atleast one equation)
  5. Solve System of Equations for current
  6. Calculate voltages using Ohm's law

Let's look at an example

Find the voltage and current in each resistor

$R_1 = 1k\Omega, R_2 = 500 \Omega, R_3 = 2 k\Omega, R_4 = 1 k\Omega$

There are 3 loops in this circuit and 4 nodes

Now these resistors don't have polarity assigned to it

That means we need to assign polarity to each resistor

Now it doesn't matter which side has the positive or negative

All that matters is that you stick with it

If your signs are backwards you will just get negative voltage, which is fine

The way to assign polarity is assume current is coming out of the positive of the battery

The current enters the positive of the resistor

Now we can make KVL and KCL equations

We have 4 unknowns (currents through each resistor)

So we need (at least) 4 equations

The way we make KVL equations is start somewhere on the loop

Now go clockwise or counterclockwise around the loop

When entering the positive side of a component you add

When entering the negative side of a component you subtract

For the 2 loops involving the $5V$ I'm going to start above the $5V$ and go CCW

For the loop without the $5V$ I'm going to start above $R_2$ and go CW

$$\begin{align} 5 - 2000I_3 - 500I_2 - 1000I_1 = 0 \\ 5 - 2000I_3 - 1000I_4 -1000I_1 = 0 \\ 1000I_4 - 500I_2 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

For KCL equations you follow the current flow

Since we have 4 nodes we can make 4 equations

But 2 of the nodes are dependent on the voltage source

We don't know the current of the voltage source, so we only have 2 equations

$$\begin{align} I_1 = I_2 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_4 = I_3\\ \end{align}$$

So we have 5 total equations, but 4 unknowns, so we can get rid of one of them

$$\begin{align} 5 - 2000I_3 - 500I_2 - 1000I_1 = 0 \\ 1000I_4 - 500I_2 = 0 \\ I_1 = I_2 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_4 = I_3 \\ \end{align}$$

If we rewrite this into a more traditional looking system

$$\begin{align} 1000I_1 + 500I_2 + 2000I_3 + 0I_4 = 5 \\ 0I_1 - 500I_2 + 0I_3 + 1000I_4 = 0 \\ I_1 - I_2 + 0I_3 -0I_4 = 0\\ 0I_1 + I_2 - I_3 + I_4 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

And as a matrix,

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1000 & 500 & 2000 & 0 \\ 0 & -500 & 0 & 1000 \\ 1 & -1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} I_1 \\ I_2 \\ I_3 \\ I_4 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

Now you can solve this system using whatever method you want, I'll just skip it here

After solving we get

$$\begin{align} I_1 = .0015 A &= 1.5 mA \\ I_2 = .001 A &= 1 mA \\ I_3 = .0015 A &= 1.5 mA \\ I_4 = .0005 A &= 0.5 mA \\ \end{align}$$

Now to get voltages, we can use Ohm's law $V=IR$

$$\begin{align} V_1 = I_1 R_1 = .0015 * 1000 = 1.5 V \\ V_2 = I_2 R_2 = .001 * 500 = 0.5 V \\ V_3 = I_3 R_3 = .0015 * 2000 = 3 V \\ V_4 = I_4 R_4 = .0005 * 1000 = 0.5 V \\ \end{align}$$
