So far we have covered Ohm's law, KCL, KVL

To recap

  • Ohm's Law: $V = IR$
  • KCL: The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum of currents leaving a node
  • KVL: The sum of voltages around any closed loop in a circuit is zero

Now we will see how to use these laws to solve circuits

What we mean by "solving circuits" is finding currents and voltages at points in a circuit

Procedure

  1. Identify all the nodes and loops in the circuit
  2. Assign polarity to all resistors if they don't have one
  3. Create KCL and KVL equations (use Ohm's law to voltage of resistors)
  4. Set up System of Equations (Make sure each unkown is in atleast one equation)
  5. Solve System of Equations for current
  6. Calculate voltages using Ohm's law

Let's look at an example

Find the voltage and current in each resistor

$R_1 = 1k\Omega, R_2 = 500 \Omega, R_3 = 2 k\Omega, R_4 = 1 k\Omega$

There are 3 loops in this circuit and 4 nodes

Now these resistors don't have polarity assigned to it

That means we need to assign polarity to each resistor

Now it doesn't matter which side has the positive or negative

All that matters is that you stick with it

If your signs are backwards you will just get negative voltage, which is fine

The way to assign polarity is assume current is coming out of the positive of the battery

The current enters the positive of the resistor

Now we can make KVL and KCL equations

We have 4 unknowns (currents through each resistor)

So we need (at least) 4 equations

The way we make KVL equations is start somewhere on the loop

Now go clockwise or counterclockwise around the loop

When entering the positive side of a component you add

When entering the negative side of a component you subtract

For the 2 loops involving the $5V$ I'm going to start above the $5V$ and go CCW

For the loop without the $5V$ I'm going to start above $R_2$ and go CW

$$\begin{align} 5 - 2000I_3 - 500I_2 - 1000I_1 = 0 \\ 5 - 2000I_3 - 1000I_4 -1000I_1 = 0 \\ 1000I_4 - 500I_2 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

For KCL equations you follow the current flow

Since we have 4 nodes we can make 4 equations

But 2 of the nodes are dependent on the voltage source

We don't know the current of the voltage source, so we only have 2 equations

$$\begin{align} I_1 = I_2 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_4 = I_3\\ \end{align}$$

So we have 5 total equations, but 4 unknowns, so we can get rid of one of them

$$\begin{align} 5 - 2000I_3 - 500I_2 - 1000I_1 = 0 \\ 1000I_4 - 500I_2 = 0 \\ I_1 = I_2 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_4 = I_3 \\ \end{align}$$

If we rewrite this into a more traditional looking system

$$\begin{align} 1000I_1 + 500I_2 + 2000I_3 + 0I_4 = 5 \\ 0I_1 - 500I_2 + 0I_3 + 1000I_4 = 0 \\ I_1 - I_2 + 0I_3 -0I_4 = 0\\ 0I_1 + I_2 - I_3 + I_4 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

And as a matrix,

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1000 & 500 & 2000 & 0 \\ 0 & -500 & 0 & 1000 \\ 1 & -1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} I_1 \\ I_2 \\ I_3 \\ I_4 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

Now you can solve this system using whatever method you want, I'll just skip it here

After solving we get

$$\begin{align} I_1 = .0015 A &= 1.5 mA \\ I_2 = .001 A &= 1 mA \\ I_3 = .0015 A &= 1.5 mA \\ I_4 = .0005 A &= 0.5 mA \\ \end{align}$$

Now to get voltages, we can use Ohm's law $V=IR$

$$\begin{align} V_1 = I_1 R_1 = .0015 * 1000 = 1.5 V \\ V_2 = I_2 R_2 = .001 * 500 = 0.5 V \\ V_3 = I_3 R_3 = .0015 * 2000 = 3 V \\ V_4 = I_4 R_4 = .0005 * 1000 = 0.5 V \\ \end{align}$$

Some Extra Details

When doing KVL, only meshes (independent loops) make independent equations

When we make systems, we need independent equations so make sure you have

enough independent equations for the unknowns

If you can't make a KCL equation because there is a voltage source,

Remember current in series is the same so you can substitute to make an independent eqn.

  • Find the Voltage And Currents of each resistor in the following circuit

    There are 6 loops and 4 nodes (but only 2 without a voltage source)

    All the possible KVL equations

    $$\begin{align} 10 - 15I_5 - 5I_4 - 10I_1 = 0 \\ 10 - 15I_5 - 15I_3 - 10I_1 = 0 \\ 10 - 15I_5 - 25I_2 - 10I_1 = 0 \\ 25I_2 - 5I_4 = 0 \\ 15I_3 - 5I_4 = 0 \\ 15I_3 - 25I_2 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

    And KCL

    $$\begin{align} I_1 = I_2 + I_3 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_3 + I_4 = I_5 \\ \end{align}$$

    We have 8 equations and 5 unknowns, so we can eliminate 3 equations

    $$\begin{align} 10 - 15I_5 - 5I_4 - 10I_1 = 0 \\ 25I_2 - 5I_4 = 0 \\ 15I_3 - 5I_4 = 0 \\ I_1 = I_2 + I_3 + I_4 \\ I_2 + I_3 + I_4 = I_5 \\ \end{align}$$

    Rewriting into standard form

    $$\begin{align} 10I_1 + 0I_2 + 0I_3 + 5I_4 + 15I_5 = 10 \\ 0I_1 -25I_2 + 0I_3 + 5I_4 + 0I_5 = 0 \\ 0I_1 + 0I_2 -15I_3 + 5I_4 + 0I_5 = 0 \\ I_1 - I_2 - I_3 - I_4 + 0I_5 = 0 \\ 0I_1 + I_2 + I_3 + I_4 - I_5 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

    As a matrix

    $$\begin{bmatrix} 10 & 0 & 0 & 5 & 15 \\ 0 & -25 & 0 & 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -15 & 5 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & -1 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & -1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} I_1 \\ I_2 \\ I_3 \\ I_4 \\ I_5 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

    After solving we get

    $$\begin{align} I_1 \approx 0.354 A &= 354 mA \\ I_2 \approx 0.046 A &= 46 mA \\ I_3 \approx 0.077 A &= 77 mA \\ I_4 \approx 0.231 A &= 231 mA \\ I_5 \approx 0.354 A &= 354 mA \\ \end{align}$$

    Now to get voltages using Ohm's law

    $$\begin{align} V_1 = I_1 R_1 = 0.354 * 10 = 3.54 V \\ V_2 = I_2 R_2 = 0.046 * 25 = 1.15 V \\ V_3 = I_3 R_3 = 0.077 * 15 = 1.155 V \\ V_4 = I_4 R_4 = 0.231 * 5 = 1.155 V \\ V_5 = I_5 R_5 = 0.354 * 15 = 5.31 V \\ \end{align}$$

  • Find the Voltage And Currents of each resistor in the following circuit

    There are 7 loops and 4 nodes (but none without a voltage source)

    For the KVL equations

    $$\begin{align} 5 + 10I_1 + 12 + 15I_4 = 0 \\ 5 + 10I_1 + 1I_2 = 0 \\ 12 + 15I_4 - 1I_2 = 0 \\ 12 - 5I_3 = 0 \\ 5I_3 + 15I_4 - 1I_2 = 0 \\ 5 + 10I_1 + 5I_3 + 15I_4 = 0 \\ 5 + 10I_1 + 12 - 5I_3 + 1I_2 = 0 \\ \end{align}$$

    We have 7 equations but only 4 unknowns so we can get rid of the extra

    Since there are no KCL equations, be careful when selecting the equations to remove

    Remember, if any rows or columns are linearly dependent, you won't get an answer

    If we convert each equation into a matrix

    $$\begin{align} 10I_1 + 0I_2 + 0I_3 + 15I_4 = -17 \\ 10I_1 + 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 0I_4 = -5 \\ 0I_1 - 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 15I_4 = -12 \\ 0I_1 + 0I_2 -5I_3 + 0I_4 = -12 \\ 0I_1 - 1I_2 + 5I_3 + 15I_4 = 0 \\ 10I_1 + 0I_2 + 5I_3 + 15I_4 = -5 \\ 10I_1 + 1I_2 -5I_3 + 0I_4 = -17 \\ \end{align}$$

    $$\begin{bmatrix} 10 & 0 & 0 & 15 \\ 10 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & 15 \\ 0 & 0 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 5 & 15 \\ 10 & 0 & 5 & 15 \\ 10 & 1 & -5 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

    We can remove the 1st, 5th, 6th,and 7th equations

    But that leaves us with 3 equation for 4 unknowns

    $$\begin{align} 10I_1 + 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 0I_4 = -5 \\ 0I_1 - 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 15I_4 = -12 \\ 0I_1 + 0I_2 -5I_3 + 0I_4 = -12 \\ \end{align}$$

    $$ \begin{bmatrix} 10 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & 15 \\ 0 & 0 & -5 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

    We can't do that

    The reason we have this issue is because we have 3 meshes

    That means we need a KCL equation to add an independent equation

    The KCL equations we make are

    $$\begin{align} I_1 = I_{5V} \\ I_2 + I_3 + I_{12V} = {I_1} \\ I_4 = I_3 + I_{12V} \\ I_{5V} = I_2 + I_4 \\ \end{align}$$

    We can plug the 3rd KCL equation into the 2nd KCL equation

    $$\begin{align} I_2 + I_4 = {I_1} \\ \end{align}$$

    This is our 4th independent equation!

    So our final system is

    $$\begin{align} 10I_1 + 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 0I_4 = -5 \\ 0I_1 - 1I_2 + 0I_3 + 15I_4 = -12 \\ 0I_1 + 0I_2 -5I_3 + 0I_4 = -12 \\ -I_1 + I_2 + 0I_3 + I_4 = 0\\ \end{align}$$

    $$\begin{bmatrix} 10 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & 15 \\ 0 & 0 & -5 & 0 \\ -1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} I_1 \\ I_2 \\ I_3 \\ I_4 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -5 \\ -12 \\ -12 \\ 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}$$

    After solving we get

    $$\begin{align} I_1 \approx -0.525 A &= -525 mA \\ I_2 \approx .257 A &= 257 mA \\ I_3 = 2.4 A &= 2400 mA \\ I_4 \approx -.782 A &= -782 mA \\ \end{align}$$

    Now to get voltages using Ohm's law

    $$\begin{align} V_1 = I_1 R_1 = -0.525 * 10 = -5.25 V \\ V_2 = I_2 R_2 = 0.257 * 1 = 0.257 V \\ V_3 = I_3 R_3 = 2.4 * 5 = 12 V \\ V_4 = I_4 R_4 = -0.782 * 15 = -11.73 V \\ \end{align}$$

  • Find the resistance of resistor R (in Ohms)

    Since current in series is the same and everything is in series

    We can find the resistance R by Ohm's law $R = \frac{10 V}{I}$

    We need to find the current I first

    We can use KVL to find I

    I'm going to assume the left of $10 \Omega$ is the positive terminal

    $$\begin{align} -20 + 10 + 10I - 30 &= 0\\ 10I &= 40 \\ I &= 4 A \\ \end{align}$$

    Now we can find R

    $$\begin{align} R &= \frac{10 V}{4 A} \\ R &= 2.5 \Omega \\ \end{align}$$
