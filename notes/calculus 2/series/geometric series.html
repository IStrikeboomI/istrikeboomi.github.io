A Geometric Series is a series in the form(s) of:

$$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{ar^n} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty{ar^{n-1}} = a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \ldots \end{align}$$

Where $a$ and $r$ are real numbers

$r$ is also commonly denote as the "common ratio"

A geometric series only converges if $|r| < 1$ (or $-1 < r < 1$)

Otherwise geometric series diverges if $|r| \geq 1$

The formula for the value of a partial and full geometric series are:

$$\begin{align} S_n &= \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r} \\ S &= \frac{a}{1-r} \end{align}$$

Deriving The Formula

$$\begin{align} S_n &= a + ar + ar^2 + \ldots + ar^{n-1} \\ rS_n &= \ \ \ \ \ \ \ ar + ar^2 + \ldots + ar^{n-1} + ar^n\\ (S_n - rS_n) &= a - ar^n \\ S_n(1 - r) &= a - ar^n \\ S_n &= \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 -r} \\ \end{align}$$

The difference between $S_n$ and $rS_n$ is the first and last term

Subtracting them only leaves the first and last term

For the full series:

If $|r| < 1$, then the limit to infinity of $r^n$ would go to $0$

$$\begin{align} \lim_{n \to \infty}{\frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 -r}} = \frac{a(1 - 0)}{1 - r} = \frac{a}{1-r} \end{align}$$

If $|r| \geq 1$, then $r^n$ goes to infinity

$$\begin{align} \lim_{n \to \infty}{\frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 -r}} = \infty \end{align}$$

It's important to know this is not the test for divergence

This is because we are the taking the limit of the value of the series, not the sequence

Examples

$$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n} = \frac{1}{ 1- 1/2} = 2 \end{align}$$

Here $a = 1$ and $r = 1/2$, and since $|1/2| < 1$, this series converges


$$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^n} = \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}} = \frac{1/3}{1 - 1/3} = \frac{1}{2} \end{align}$$

Since this series starts at $ n= 1$, we have to transform the inner part into the $ar^{n-1}$ form

And since $a = 1/3$ and $r=1/3$, this series converges

Practice Problems
  • Determine the value of $\displaystyle\sum_{n = 0}^\infty{2\frac{1}{8^n}}$

    $$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{2\frac{1}{8^n}} = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{2\left(\frac{1}{8}\right)^n} = \frac{2}{1-1/8} = \frac{16}{7} \end{align}$$

  • Determine the value of $\displaystyle\sum_{n = 1}^\infty{4\frac{2^{n-1}}{3^{2n}}}$

    $$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{4\frac{2^{n-1}}{3^{2n}}} &= \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{4\frac{2^{n-1}}{({3^2})^n}} \\ &= \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{4\frac{2^{n-1}}{9^n}} \\ &= \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{4\frac{2^{n-1}}{(9)9^{n-1}}} \\ &= \sum_{n = 1}^\infty{\frac{4}{9}\left(\frac{2}{9}\right)^{n-1}} \\ &= \frac{4/9}{1 - 2/9} \\ &= \frac{4/9}{1 - 2/9} \\ &= \frac{4}{7} \end{align}$$

  • Determine the value of $\displaystyle\sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\frac{10^n}{(-9)^{n+1}}}$

    $$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\frac{10^n}{(-9)^{n+1}}} &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\frac{10^n}{(-9)(-9)^n}} \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{-9\left(-\frac{10}{9}\right)^n} \\ \end{align}$$

    Since $-10/9 > 1$, this series diverges

  • Determine the value of $\displaystyle\sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{n-1}}$

    $$\begin{align} \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{n-1}} &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{n}} \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty{\sqrt{3}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{n}} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{1 - 1/\sqrt{3}} \\ &= \frac{3}{\sqrt{3} - 1} \\ &\approx 4.09807 \end{align}$$
